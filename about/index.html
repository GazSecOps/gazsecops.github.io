Hi, I’m Gareth#

Or Gaz to mates. British engineer specialising in security, operations, and breaking things.

What This Blog Is About#

Security operations and the messy reality of running systems in production.

Not theory. Not vendor marketing. What actually works when things go wrong at 3am.

Main topics:

  • Security operations and incident response
  • Cloud security (mostly Azure these days)
  • OAuth 2.0 and authentication (it’s harder than it looks)
  • AI systems responsibility (spoiler: most organisations can’t do it properly)
  • DevOps, containers, and infrastructure
  • Conference writeups (FOSDEM, etc.)

Background#

Years of breaking into systems professionally. Then years of defending them properly.

Making Linux. Breaking it. Using it in fintechs and other places that can’t afford things to break.

The hard way. Through incidents, post-mortems, and learning that boring tools + discipline beat fancy platforms + chaos every time.

I write about what I’ve learned running production systems, dealing with breaches, and watching smart people make dumb decisions because of organisational dysfunction.

"The system is designed to produce bad outcomes. Your job is to minimise the damage. Not prevent it entirely. Minimise it."

Philosophy#

  • Security isn’t a checklist — it’s understanding the system and identifying where it can be broken
  • Monitoring accuracy matters more than monitoring uptime
  • Build political capital and spend it when you need to say no
  • Use boring proven tools over AI-specific platforms
  • Be honest about what doesn’t work (spoiler: most things don’t)
